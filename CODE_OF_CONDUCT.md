# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the AI Threat Hunting Simulator community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on advancing cybersecurity education and threat detection capabilities.

## Ethical Use Policy

This project is designed for **defensive security**, **education**, and **research purposes only**. By participating in this community, you agree to:

- Use the simulator for security training and education
- Develop and improve threat detection capabilities
- Research attack patterns in safe, controlled environments
- Share knowledge to improve defensive security posture
- Contribute scenarios that help security teams prepare for real threats
- Test and validate security controls in authorized environments

- Use generated telemetry or techniques for malicious purposes
- Apply knowledge gained to conduct unauthorized penetration testing
- Deploy attack scenarios against systems you don't own or have permission to test
- Circumvent security controls without proper authorization
- Share information that could enable malicious activity
- Distribute malware, exploits, or attack tools outside the educational context

**Violation of ethical use policies will result in immediate removal from the community and may be reported to appropriate authorities.**

## Our Standards

### Examples of Positive Behavior

**Contributing to a Positive Environment:**
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community and security education
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Sharing knowledge to help others learn
- Acknowledging and crediting contributions from others

**Technical Excellence:**
- Writing clean, well-documented code
- Providing thorough testing for contributions
- Following established coding standards
- Being responsive to code reviews and feedback
- Helping to maintain code quality

**Community Engagement:**
- Asking questions and helping others
- Participating in discussions constructively
- Providing detailed bug reports and feature requests
- Offering to mentor newcomers
- Celebrating successes and milestones

### Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

**Harassment and Discrimination:**
- Violent threats or language directed against another person
- Discriminatory jokes and language
- Posting sexually explicit or violent material
- Personal insults, particularly those related to protected characteristics
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behavior

**Disruptive Behavior:**
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Spam, excessive self-promotion, or off-topic discussions
- Deliberately derailing conversations or discussions

**Unethical Technical Practices:**
- Sharing malicious code or exploits
- Contributing backdoors or vulnerabilities intentionally
- Misrepresenting the purpose or capabilities of contributions
- Plagiarizing code or scenarios without attribution
- Violating software licenses or intellectual property rights

## Security Researcher Responsibilities

As a community focused on cybersecurity, we hold ourselves to high ethical standards:

### Responsible Disclosure

If you discover a security vulnerability in this project:

1. **Do NOT** open a public GitHub issue
2. **Do** email security details to the maintainers privately
3. **Do** allow reasonable time for fixes before public disclosure
4. **Do** work collaboratively with maintainers to address the issue

### Scenario Development Ethics

When creating attack scenarios:

1. **Base on real-world, publicly-documented attacks** - Don't create novel 0-days
2. **Use synthetic data only** - Never include real credentials, IPs, or PII
3. **Mark dangerous patterns clearly** - Help users understand risks
4. **Provide defensive context** - Explain how to detect and prevent each attack
5. **Follow responsible disclosure** - If based on recent vulnerabilities, ensure patches exist

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Email communications with maintainers
- Social media when representing the project
- Community events (virtual or in-person)
- Any other official project channels

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces, such as:

- Posting via an official social media account
- Acting as an appointed representative at events

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

Instances of abusive, harassing, unethical, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

**Email:** [security@project-domain.com]

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

When reporting, please include:

- Your contact information
- Names (usernames) of individuals involved
- Description of the incident
- Approximate date/time
- Any relevant links, screenshots, or evidence
- Any additional context

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

### Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

#### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

#### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior or unethical use of project resources.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

#### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including:
- Sustained inappropriate behavior
- Harassment of individuals
- Malicious or unethical use of project resources
- Violation of ethical use policies

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community. Contributions may be removed or attributed appropriately.

### Escalation Path

1. **Minor Issues**: Handled by maintainers with a warning
2. **Moderate Issues**: Temporary restrictions or bans
3. **Severe Issues**: Immediate permanent ban + potential legal action for criminal behavior
4. **Ethical Violations**: Immediate removal + notification to appropriate authorities if applicable
